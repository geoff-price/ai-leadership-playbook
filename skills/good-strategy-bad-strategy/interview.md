# Interview flow — Good Strategy / Bad Strategy

Run this before producing anything. Goal: find the reader's REAL diagnosis and strip the
fluff. Ask one question at a time; adapt follow-ups. Be sharp — respect their time.

## Core questions

1. **The initiative.** What AI decision, roadmap, or initiative are you trying to get
   right? (One sentence.)
2. **The stakes.** What happens if it goes wrong — cost, wasted quarters, credibility,
   risk?
3. **The slogan.** What's the version you'd put on a leadership slide today? (Paste it.
   We're going to test it.)
4. **The real obstacle.** Forget the goal for a second. What is actually in the way of the
   outcome — adoption, data, trust, governance, a specific painful workflow, talent? What's
   your evidence it's that and not something else?
5. **Strength vs. weakness.** What genuine strength could you point at that obstacle? Where
   is the most promising opening? (Strategy is strength applied to weakness — name both.)
6. **Coherence check.** List the moves currently planned. Do they reinforce each other, or
   are they a grab-bag pulling in different directions for the same budget?

## Closing question (always)

7. **How will you use this?** A strategy-kernel one-pager, talking points for a meeting, a
   working doc, or a dashboard? (Determines the format. If unsure, I'll recommend the
   one-pager.)

## After the interview

Produce the artifact for their stated use:
- A clear **diagnosis** (the real challenge, in one or two sentences).
- A **guiding policy** (the approach, not a task list).
- 3–5 **coherent actions** that carry out the policy and reinforce each other.
- A short **bad-strategy flags** section noting any fluff, unfaced challenges, or
  goals-masquerading-as-strategy you caught.

End with: one honest flag of where their thinking is still weak, and a one-line nudge to
read the book for the parts that run deeper than this tool (e.g. sources of advantage,
proximate objectives, chain-link systems).
