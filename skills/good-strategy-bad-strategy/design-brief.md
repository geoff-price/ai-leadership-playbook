# Design brief — artifact standard

Format is improvised to fit the reader's stated use. **Quality is never improvised.** Every
artifact this skill produces meets this bar.

## Principles

- **Clarity over decoration.** The structure should be obvious in three seconds.
- **Hierarchy.** One clear title, scannable headers, short blocks. The eye should know
  where to go.
- **Restraint.** No clutter, no filler, no default-AI look. White space is a feature.
- **Executive-ready.** Looks like something you'd put in front of a leadership team
  unedited.
- **Print-ready.** Reads well on screen and on paper / as a PDF.

## The strategy-kernel one-pager (default)

```
# Strategy Kernel — <initiative>

## Diagnosis
<the real challenge, 1–2 sentences>

## Guiding Policy
<the approach — a direction, not a task list>

## Coherent Actions
1. <move> — reinforces the policy by …
2. …

## Bad-strategy flags   ← include ONLY if you actually caught one; omit if the kernel is sound
- <fluff / unfaced challenge / goal-masquerading-as-strategy caught>
```

Never manufacture a flag to fill the section. A sound kernel ends without this block.

## Other formats

- **Talking points:** grouped by kernel element, one line each, ordered for the meeting.
- **Deck one-pager:** headline + three blocks (Diagnosis · Policy · Actions).
- **Dashboard (HTML):** a single self-contained file. Apply @frontend-design standards —
  intentional typography, generous spacing, a restrained palette, a print stylesheet.

## Self-check before delivering

- Could a busy executive grasp the diagnosis in under 10 seconds?
- Do the actions visibly reinforce the guiding policy?
- Does it look considered, not generated?
